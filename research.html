Research

Perovskite solar modules, scalable processing, and flexible optoelectronics.

I work across fabrication, simulation, interconnection design, and materials characterization to support efficient large-area photovoltaic devices.

Selected Work

Projects

Thin-Film Deposition

Blade-coating design and CFD-assisted scale-up

Led fluid-dynamics optimization for scalable deposition of perovskite and HTL films using customized blade architectures and Ansys Fluent simulations.

Module Integration

Laser patterning for large-area PSMs

Optimized P1, P2, and P3 laser scribing to reduce dead-zone width, suppress shunt leakage, and improve geometric fill factor in monolithic modules.

Microfabrication

Micropyramid PDMS anti-reflection layers

Developed photolithography and soft-lithography protocols for structured light-management layers using CAD masks, ICP etching, KOH etching, and PDMS molding.

Flexible Electronics

Solar-driven self-powered OECT system

Designed an integrated sensing platform coupling flexible perovskite solar cells with organic electrochemical transistors and rollable polyimide-based interconnects.

Methods

From process design to device validation.

Process and simulation

Scalable device fabrication with spin coating, blade coating, PVD, e-beam evaporation, photolithography, mask design, and laser scribing. Simulation experience includes Ansys Fluent CFD, SCAPS-1D, and Silvaco TCAD.

Characterization

Carrier and optoelectronic analysis with SCLC, PL, TRPL, EIS, Mott-Schottky, admittance, J-V, EQE, SPO, and ISOS reliability standards; materials analysis with SEM, AFM, XRD, TEM, XPS, ToF-SIMS, UPS, Raman, UV-Vis, and KPFM.
